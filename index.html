Hemochromatosis Awareness

July Is National Hemochromatosis Awareness Month!

What is Hemochromatosis?

Hereditary hemochromatosis causes your body to absorb too much iron from the food you eat. Excess iron is stored in your organs, especially your liver, heart and pancreas.
Too much iron can lead to life-threatening conditions, such as liver disease, heart problems and diabetes.

What Are Symptoms of Hemochromatosis Disease?

Symptoms are related to conditions that arise from iron overload such as diabetes, darkening of the skin, abnormal heart rhythm, or arthritis.
